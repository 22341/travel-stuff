## Key Highlights

### Cultural Immersion
- **Riga:** Art Nouveau architecture and Latvian heritage
- **Tartu:** Estonia's intellectual heart with vibrant student culture
- **Tallinn:** Medieval architecture and UNESCO heritage
- **Craft beer scene:** Latvian and Estonian microbreweries and local gastropubs

### Natural Beauty
- **Toomemägi Hill:** Green sanctuary in Tartu's city centre
- **Kadriorg Park:** Baroque palace grounds in Tallinn

### Historical Significance
- **Riga Old Town:** UNESCO World Heritage medieval and Art Nouveau architecture
- **House of the Blackheads:** Iconic Riga landmark
- **University of Tartu:** One of Northern Europe's oldest universities (founded 1632)
- **Tallinn Old Town:** Best-preserved medieval city in Northern Europe
- **Soviet heritage:** KGB Museum and post-Soviet transformation

## Travel Notes

### Transportation
- **Early morning flight:** Arrive at Leeds Bradford Airport by 4:00am for 6:00am departure
- **Direct train connections:** New direct rail service from Riga to Tartu, then continuing to Tallinn
- **Train travel:** Comfortable and scenic routes through the Baltic countryside
- **Return flight:** Via Amsterdam with KLM, allowing time for connection

### Practical Tips
- **Visas:** Not required for UK citizens visiting Estonia/Latvia (check current requirements)
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR) used in both countries
- **Language:** Estonian and Latvian are official languages; English widely spoken in tourist areas and by younger generations
- **Weather:** March temperatures typically 0-5°C; pack warm layers and waterproof clothing
- **Accommodation:** All hotels confirmed and centrally located near Old Town areas and craft beer scenes
- **Time zone:** EET (Eastern European Time), 2 hours ahead of UK
